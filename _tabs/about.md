## Hi, I'm Luís

I'm transitioning into cybersecurity with a focus on **offensive security and penetration testing**. This site is where I'm documenting that journey publicly — every CTF box I crack, every web vulnerability I learn to exploit, every misconfiguration I find — alongside the dead ends and the lessons that don't usually make it into polished writeups.

I publish under the handle **ciberacaro** — a play on *ciber* and *ácaro* (Portuguese for "mite": small, persistent, hard to shake off).

## Why this site exists

There is a gap between *learning* security and *demonstrating* security. Reading books, watching videos, and finishing CTF rooms doesn't show anyone what you can actually do. This portfolio is my answer to that gap.

The concrete plan, over the next ~6 months:

- Earn the **eJPT** certification
- Build a consistent body of **CTF writeups** — HackTheBox, TryHackMe, PortSwigger Web Security Academy
- Publish a few **small tools and utilities** I write along the way
- Land a first job in **penetration testing**, in Portugal or remote-friendly

## Where I'm starting from

I'd rather be transparent than perform expertise I don't have. Here is the honest baseline as of when this site went up:

- **Linux:** can navigate a terminal and run common tools
- **Networking:** the fundamentals — TCP/IP, common protocols, subnetting, basic packet analysis

If you're a recruiter or hiring manager: I'm not at the level of someone with two years of pentesting under their belt. I'm at the level of someone who is going to be doing the work daily for the next several months and intends to be employable at the end of it. The commit history of this site will reflect that work in real time.

## Approach

Three principles guide what I publish here:

1. **Quality over volume.** A well-written writeup that explains the *reasoning* is worth more than ten that only list commands.
2. **Show the path, not just the destination.** "I tried X, it failed because Y, so I tried Z" is more valuable than "I ran this command and got root."
3. **Consistency over intensity.** Weekly commits beat a one-month sprint followed by six months of silence.
